1st Day of Lab Attendence Policy

It has been, and will remain, the policy of the Department of Chemistry that a student may be removed from a class list and be replaced with persons on the wait list at the beginning of the first laboratory period.


  • At the beginning of the first lab period, the Department's lab manager organizes the students present in teams of two using waitlisted students if registered students are not present.

  • At this point, for safety concerns, anyone on the wait list who has not gained access to the lab is asked to leave.

  • The lab manager then assigns Lab kits to the students in courses that uses kits (note: General Chemistry no longer uses kits). The Lab kits are checked and signed off by the student for completeness and safety.

  • The lab manager then initiates departmental, University, state, and federally mandated safety training.


  • Immediately following the training, students are must sign off that they have been made aware of the safety issues when taking a laboratory course.

  • The above tasks for the lab manager are exacerbated by the fact that the lab manager is attempting this task in multiple, simultaneously run lab sections.

  • In addition, after the described activities, most lab sections are then required to complete their first laboratory experiment in the time remaining.
